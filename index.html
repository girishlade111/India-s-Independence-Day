A Glimpse into History

The long walk to freedom.

1857: The First War of Independence

Often called the Sepoy Mutiny, this was a major uprising against the rule of the British East India Company, marking a significant turning point in the struggle for freedom.

1885: Formation of Indian National Congress

The INC was founded to create a platform for civil and political dialogue among educated Indians, which later became the principal leader of the independence movement.

1920: Non-Cooperation Movement

Led by Mahatma Gandhi, this was one of the first mass movements of civil disobedience, urging Indians to withdraw their labor from any activity that sustained the British government.

1930: Dandi March (Salt March)

An act of nonviolent civil disobedience led by Gandhi to protest the British salt tax. This 24-day, 240-mile march triggered a wider civil disobedience movement across India.

1942: Quit India Movement

A movement launched by Gandhi demanding an end to British rule in India. It was a call for determined, but passive resistance that signified the boiling point of Indian frustration.

1947: Independence & Partition

On August 15, 1947, India achieved independence from British rule. The Indian Independence Act 1947 partitioned British India into the two new independent Dominions of India and Pakistan.

Architects of Freedom

Remembering the heroes who sacrificed everything for our nation.

Mahatma Gandhi

The father of the nation, who led India to independence through nonviolent civil disobedience.

Sardar Patel

The "Iron Man of India," instrumental in integrating the princely states into the Indian union.

Jawaharlal Nehru

The first Prime Minister of India, a central figure in Indian politics before and after independence.

Subhas Chandra Bose

A prominent nationalist leader who founded the All India Forward Bloc and led the Indian National Army.

Bhagat Singh

A charismatic revolutionary who became a symbol of resistance and inspired thousands of youths.

Rani Lakshmibai

The Queen of Jhansi, one of the leading figures of the 1857 rebellion and a symbol of bravery.

B.R. Ambedkar

Architect of the Indian Constitution, a jurist, economist, and social reformer.

Sarojini Naidu

The "Nightingale of India," a distinguished poet, and the first Indian woman to be president of the INC.

Legendary Indian Kings

Icons of sovereignty and courage.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

A visionary king and the founder of the Maratha Empire. Revered for his guerrilla warfare tactics, administrative skills, and for championing 'Hindavi Swarajya' (Self-Rule of Hindu people).

Maharana Pratap

The valiant Rajput king of Mewar, known for his indomitable spirit and resistance against the Mughal expansion. His name is synonymous with courage, sacrifice, and the fight for honor.
